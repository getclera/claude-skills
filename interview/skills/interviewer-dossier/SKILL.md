# Interviewer Dossier (from LinkedIn)

## What This Skill Does

Reads an interviewer's **exported LinkedIn profile (PDF)** and produces a tactical profile to help the
candidate connect with them: their career arc, what they're genuinely expert in, what they probably
care about in a hire, where their knowledge likely runs deep vs. thin, and concrete ways to build
rapport and tailor answers.

The goal isn't to "stalk" — it's the prep a sharp candidate does anyway, done thoroughly. Knowing
your interviewer's background lets you speak their language and aim your answers at what they value.

---

## Gather Maximum Context First

1. **The interviewer's LinkedIn profile, exported as PDF** (required). Tell the candidate how:
   on the interviewer's LinkedIn profile → "More" button → "Save to PDF" → upload it here.
2. **The role being interviewed for** (required) — the dossier is useless without knowing what the
   candidate is being assessed for.
3. **Helpful extras** — the interviewer's title/role on the panel (hiring manager? future peer?
   skip-level? cross-functional?), and the interview format. How you play to a future peer differs
   from how you play to the decision-maker.

If you only get the PDF with no role context, ask for the role before producing the dossier.

---

## Process

1. **Read the LinkedIn PDF** for: career trajectory, current role and scope, past companies and what
   they'd have learned at each, education, skills they highlight, posts/articles if included,
   tenure patterns, and how they describe themselves.
2. **Infer what they know deeply** — domains where their experience is genuinely deep, where they'll
   ask sharp questions and detect bluffing.
3. **Infer what they likely care about in a hire** — based on their own path (e.g., a former founder
   values scrappiness; a long-tenured operator values reliability; an IC-turned-manager values craft).
4. **Map their likely blind spots** — areas outside their experience where the candidate's depth could
   shine, or where the interviewer may need things explained more simply. (Use this to calibrate, not
   to condescend.)
5. **Produce rapport hooks** — genuine common ground or shared interests, and how to reference them
   naturally.

> **Honesty rule:** Everything is inference from a public profile, clearly flagged as inference. Don't
> state guesses as fact. Don't fabricate detail the PDF doesn't support. Keep it professional — this
> is interview prep, not surveillance; stick to career-relevant signal.

---

## Output Format

```
# Your Interviewer: [Name], [Title]
*(All inferred from their LinkedIn profile — treat as informed hypotheses, not facts.)*

## Their Story (the 30-second read)
[Career arc in a few lines. Where they've been, where they are, what shaped them.]

## What They Know Cold
[Domains where they're deep. Where they'll ask sharp questions and spot bluffing — so be precise here.]

## What They Probably Value in a Hire
[Inferred from their own path. What will resonate with this specific person.]

## Where You Can Shine / Calibrate
[Areas outside their core experience — where your depth stands out, or where to explain rather than
assume. Framed as calibration, not condescension.]

## Rapport Hooks
[Genuine common ground — shared background, interests, companies, schools. How to bring it up
naturally, not forced.]

## How to Aim Your Answers at This Person
[2–3 concrete adjustments to make given who they are.]
```

---

## Quality Bar / Failure Checklist

This dossier FAILS if it:
- ❌ States inferences as facts instead of flagging them as hypotheses
- ❌ Fabricates detail the LinkedIn PDF doesn't support
- ❌ Strays from career-relevant signal into anything that reads as surveillance
- ❌ Produces generic advice not tied to this specific interviewer's background
- ❌ Reads as condescending about the interviewer's "blind spots" instead of using them to calibrate
- ❌ Skips the role context, making the prep un-targeted
