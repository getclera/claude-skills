# Strengths & Gaps Positioning

## The Framing (Read This First)

If the candidate got the interview, the company already believes they *can* do the role. So this is
**not** a "can you do this job" filter. The job here is:
1. **Find what they're genuinely strong at** for this role and help them highlight it hard.
2. **Surface where they're thin** and either pull out a real story they undervalue, or coach them to
   build a credible narrative — honestly.

Never tell a candidate they can't do the role. Help them present the best true version of themselves.

---

## The Core Technique: Probe for Specifics, Don't Ask Generically

The key move that makes this skill good: when a JD names a specific requirement, **don't ask the
candidate a yes/no version of it. Ask specific questions that reveal whether they've actually done
it.**

**Example — a PM role requires "taken a product from 0→1 and from 1→10":**
- ❌ Weak: "Have you taken a product from 0 to 1?" (invites a reflexive "yes")
- ✅ Strong: "Tell me about a product you started from nothing — what was the first version, who were
  the first users, how did you decide what to build? Then: how did it grow after that — what changed
  between the early version and when it scaled, and what did *you* specifically do to drive that?"

The specific questions surface the truth. Then:
- **If they've genuinely done it** → "This is a major strength for this role. Here's exactly how to
  highlight it: [framing]."
- **If they haven't** → "I think you need a stronger story here. Here's an angle from what you *have*
  done that's adjacent and credible: [pitch a real, honest story]." Never fabricate; build from true
  material.

---

## Gather Maximum Context First

1. **The job description** (required) — pasted in full, so the specific requirements are visible.
2. **The candidate's background** (required) — resume uploaded, plus willingness to answer probing
   questions. The resume alone isn't enough; the probing is where the signal is.
3. **The company/role context** — what the team is, stage, anything they know about priorities.

Tell the candidate up front: "I'm going to ask you some specific questions about your experience —
the more concretely you answer, the better I can help you position yourself."

---

## Process

1. **Extract every real requirement** from the JD — including the implied ones (a "0→1 and 1→10"
   line is really two distinct requirements).
2. **For each requirement, probe with specific questions** (per the technique above) to find out
   whether the candidate has true, demonstrable experience.
3. **Sort into strengths vs. gaps** based on their answers, not on the resume's surface.
4. **For strengths:** coach exactly how to highlight each — the story structure, the specific detail
   to lead with, where to deploy it in the interview.
5. **For gaps:** pull out an honest adjacent story if one exists, or coach a credible "here's how I'd
   approach it / here's the closest thing I've done" narrative. Flag anything that's a genuine stretch
   so they're not caught off guard.
6. **Assemble their positioning** — the 3–4 things to lead with, and how to handle the thin spots.

---

## Output Format

```
# Positioning for [Role] at [Company]

## Your Real Strengths (lead with these)
For each: **[Requirement]** → [the true experience that matches] → [exactly how to highlight it].

## Where You're Thinner (get ahead of these)
For each: **[Requirement]** → [honest read from your answers] → [the adjacent story to use, OR the
credible narrative to build]. Flagged: [anything that's a genuine stretch].

## Questions I Still Need You to Answer
[Any requirement where the candidate's answer was vague — ask the specific follow-ups before
finalizing positioning.]

## Your Interview Pitch (the through-line)
[The 3–4 strengths woven into a coherent "why I'm right for this" narrative.]
```

---

## Quality Bar / Failure Checklist

This FAILS if it:
- ❌ Tells the candidate they can't do the role (wrong framing — they got the interview)
- ❌ Asks generic yes/no versions of requirements instead of specific, revealing questions
- ❌ Accepts a surface "yes" without probing for real evidence
- ❌ Fabricates experience to fill a gap instead of building from true material
- ❌ Doesn't flag genuine stretches, leaving the candidate exposed
- ❌ Works from the resume alone without the probing conversation that surfaces real signal
