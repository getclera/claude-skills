# Hiring Manager Brief Creator

## What This Skill Does

Produces the **intake brief** that should exist before any search starts — the document that aligns
the recruiter and hiring manager on *exactly* who they're looking for, how they'll find them, and how
they'll evaluate. Most bad searches fail at intake: the recruiter sources against a fuzzy target and
wastes weeks. This skill forces the target into focus up front.

---

## Process

1. **Gather the role context:** title, team, why the role exists, what success looks like in 6
   months, who it reports to, comp band, location/work type.
2. **Define the ideal candidate profile:** must-haves vs. nice-to-haves (kept honest and short), the
   *shape* of the right person (background, slope, the problem they've solved before), and explicit
   anti-patterns (who looks right but isn't).
3. **Draft the sourcing strategy:** where this person likely is, search angles, target companies,
   outreach approach.
4. **Outline the interview plan:** the loop, who's on the panel, what each stage assesses (tie to a
   rubric), whether a work trial fits.
5. **Set the timeline:** target dates for shortlist, first interviews, decision.

---

## How to Write a Good Brief

- **Force the target into focus.** The value is precision. "Someone who's done growth at an early
  marketing-tech company and adapts fast" beats "a strong growth person."
- **Name the anti-patterns.** Defining who looks right but *isn't* (e.g., "someone who'll just
  replay a prior playbook") saves more time than the must-have list.
- **Keep must-haves honest.** Every fake requirement narrows the funnel and slows the search.
- **Tie evaluation to a rubric** so the brief connects straight to scoring.
- **Make it a working doc**, not a formality — the recruiter should be able to start sourcing
  *directly* off it.

---

## Output Format

```
# Hiring Brief: [Role]
**Team · Reports to · Comp band · Location/type · Why now**

## Success in 6 months
[What this person will have accomplished if it's working.]

## Ideal Candidate Profile
**Must-haves (honest, short):** ...
**Nice-to-haves:** ...
**The shape:** [background / slope / problem they've solved before]
**Anti-patterns (looks right, isn't):** ...

## Sourcing Strategy
[Where they are, target companies, search angles, outreach approach.]

## Interview Plan
| Stage | Who | Assesses (rubric dimension) |
|---|---|---|
[Note whether a work trial fits.]

## Timeline
[Shortlist by · First interviews by · Decision by]
```

---

## Brief Failure Checklist

A brief FAILS if it:
- ❌ Leaves the target fuzzy ("a strong X") instead of precise
- ❌ Omits anti-patterns, the highest-leverage part of intake
- ❌ Pads must-haves with fake requirements that narrow the funnel
- ❌ Has no sourcing angle the recruiter can act on immediately
- ❌ Defines an interview loop with no link to how candidates get scored
- ❌ Has no timeline, so the search drifts
